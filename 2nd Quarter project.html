The Chocolate Hills are a geological formation of as many as 1,776 conical hills in Bohol, spread over 50 sq km across the towns of Carmen, Batuan, and Sagbayan. The hills are covered in green grass that turns brown during the dry season of February to June, thus the reason why it is called "Chocolate Hills"

Quirino Bridge, is located between two beautiful mountains and is built to honor then-President Elpidio Quirino. This old bridge, is an iconic symbol of Ilocos Sur, it is now currently preserved as a tourist attraction in Illocos Sur.

Intramuros,Latin for “inside the walls”, is a historic walled area constructed by the Spanish Imperial Government in the late 16th century. The walls was made to protect the area from invasions. During the Battle of Manila during World War II, Intramuros was badly destroyed by the Japanese Imperial Army.
